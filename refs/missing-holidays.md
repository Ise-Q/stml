# Diagnosing Missing Dates in a Daily Futures OHLCV Dataset (1990–2022)

## TL;DR
- **Most missing weekdays are exchange holidays**, but ~9 hard-to-explain categories remain. Use `exchange_calendars` (`CMES` for NYMEX/COMEX/CME-floor, `XEUR` for Eurex/FESX) plus `pandas_market_calendars.CME_Equity` for ES/NQ, and fall back to a hand-coded holiday table for 1990–1999 because the library calendars are only reliable from roughly 2000 onward.
- **The single most powerful diagnostic is the cross-sectional presence matrix**: if a date is missing for every instrument on a given venue, it is almost certainly a market-wide holiday; if it is missing for only one or two, the cause is exchange-specific holiday, pre-inception, contract roll, low liquidity, or a vendor artifact (the Sunday 2005-05-08 rows for gc/hg/si are textbook examples).
- **Beyond holidays**, expect: instrument inception gaps (es 1997-09, fesx 1998-06, nq 1999-06), the 9/11 NYMEX closure (Sept 11 through Sept 14 — per EIA Natural Gas Weekly Update, week of Sept 17, 2001: "Futures trading did not resume until Friday, and then only in an abbreviated session using the NYMEX ACCESS system. Regular, open-outcry trading resumed at around 11:30 AM today (Monday, September 17)"), Reagan funeral 2004-06-11, Ford funeral 2007-01-02 (NYMEX/COMEX electronic continued, so CL/GC may *not* be missing), Hurricane Sandy 2012-10-29/30 (ES/NQ definitely missing, CL/GC possibly present), Bush funeral 2018-12-05 (ES/NQ missing; CL/GC ambiguous), and Eurex-only closures (Easter Monday, May 1, Dec 26).

## Key Findings

### 1. Library landscape and recommendation
Three Python libraries cover this problem. After comparison, the recommended stack is **`exchange_calendars` + `pandas_market_calendars`**, with a **hand-coded fallback** for 1990–1999.

| Library | Strength | Weakness | Use for |
|---|---|---|---|
| `pandas_market_calendars` (v5.x) | Mirrors all `exchange_calendars` calendars; adds purpose-built futures calendars (`CME_Equity`, `CME_Bond`, `CME_Agriculture`, `CMEGlobex_Grains`); models early closes (`special_closes`) and interruptions; NYSE calendar is reliable from 1885 | No dedicated NYMEX/COMEX/energy calendar — energies and metals must use the generic `CMES` mirror | ES, NQ via `CME_Equity`; everything else via `CMES` |
| `exchange_calendars` (v4.x, formerly `trading_calendars`) | Has `CMES` (unified CME Group MIC group XCME/XCBT/XNYM/XCEC) and `XEUR` (Eurex) | `CMES` does **not** distinguish NYMEX/COMEX from CME — pre-2008 NYMEX-specific schedules (e.g., NYMEX floor open on MLK Day in the 1990s) are not modeled; default `bound_min` for `CMES`/`XEUR` is roughly 2000 | Authoritative source for `CMES`, `XEUR` 2000–2022 |
| `holidays` (PyPI `holidays`) | `holidays.NYSE()` and `holidays.financial_holidays('NYSE')` give labelled US-equity holidays; `holidays.ECB()` for TARGET2 / Euro bond calendar | Only equity/bond, no futures-venue calendars; only one US market (`NYSE`) and one euro market (`ECB`); does not model early closes | Cheap, dependable **labels** for holiday *names* (e.g., "Memorial Day") to attach to a date |

**Calendar codes you will actually pass to the libraries:**
- `mcal.get_calendar("NYSE")` — NYSE; reliable 1885→present (good fallback for US business days back to 1990).
- `mcal.get_calendar("CME_Equity")` — CMEEquityExchangeCalendar; the correct choice for ES (E-mini S&P 500) and NQ (E-mini Nasdaq-100). Has its own holiday rules that differ from NYSE (NYSE may close while CME has an early close, and vice versa).
- `mcal.get_calendar("CMES")` (a mirror of `exchange_calendars.get_calendar("CMES")`) — the unified CME Group calendar; the best available proxy for NYMEX energies (CL/HO/RB/NG) and COMEX metals (GC/SI/HG/PL). Aliases include `CME`, `CBOT`, `COMEX`, `NYMEX` → all dispatch to `CMES`.
- `mcal.get_calendar("XEUR")` — Eurex; used for FESX (Euro STOXX 50).

**Install:** `pip install pandas-market-calendars exchange-calendars holidays`. As of `pandas_market_calendars` 5.3.2 and `exchange_calendars` 4.13.2 (May 2026), all four calendar codes resolve.

**Known coverage limitation that matters for this dataset:** the `CMES` and `XEUR` calendars in `exchange_calendars` were originally written for Quantopian/Zipline and have default `bound_min` near 2000-01-01. Holidays in 1990–1999 generated by `CMES.sessions_in_range()` are not guaranteed accurate (in particular, the library typically does **not** distinguish NYMEX's pre-2008 behavior of trading energy on MLK Day and Presidents Day). The code below therefore augments the calendar output with a hardcoded US futures holiday list covering 1990–1999.

### 2. The U.S. futures holiday schedule (1990–2022)
CME Group's holiday schedule explicitly tracks NYSE/SIFMA: *"Holiday schedules are subject to input from the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and the Securities Industry and Financial Markets Association (SIFMA)"* (cmegroup.com/trading-hours.html). The recurring full-day closures are:

1. **New Year's Day** (Jan 1, observed prior Friday or following Monday if on weekend).
2. **Martin Luther King Jr. Day** (3rd Monday of January). MLK Day became a federal holiday in 1986 but was **first observed as a U.S. exchange holiday in 1998**, per Trade Ideas, "MLK Day & the Stock Market" (Jan 16, 2026): *"MLK Day became a federal holiday in 1986, but the NYSE and NASDAQ didn't begin observing it until 1998."* CME/NYMEX aligned in or around 1998 as well; treat pre-1998 January 15–21 Mondays as **trading days**.
3. **Washington's Birthday / Presidents' Day** (3rd Monday of February).
4. **Good Friday**. Full closure for CME Group including NYMEX/COMEX: *"This is the big one. No trading at all on Friday"* (crosstrade.io/blog/cme-trading-hours-2026).
5. **Memorial Day** (last Monday of May).
6. **Juneteenth** (June 19, observed Friday or Monday if weekend). First observed by NYSE/CME in **2022**, per Bloomberg, "What Is Juneteenth? How Did It Become a US Holiday?" (June 13, 2022): *"The New York Stock Exchange and the Securities Industry and Financial Markets Association began observing Juneteenth as a market holiday in 2022."* 2021's Juneteenth was **not** an exchange closure. In 2022 CME treated Monday June 20 as an early-close/modified-hours day rather than a full closure for several products — *expect a settle row to exist for June 20, 2022* for many CL/GC instruments but a missing/abbreviated row for ES/NQ.
7. **Independence Day** (July 4, observed Friday or Monday if weekend).
8. **Labor Day** (1st Monday of September).
9. **Thanksgiving Day** (4th Thursday of November).
10. **Christmas Day** (Dec 25, observed Friday or Monday if weekend).

**Early closes (half-days)** — these do **not** cause a missing row, only a lower volume / earlier-than-usual settlement timestamp. The standard set is: the day after Thanksgiving (Black Friday, 1:00 p.m. ET close), Christmas Eve (1:00 p.m. ET), and July 3 when July 4 falls on a weekday (1:00 p.m. ET). For CME futures, the day before a holiday often has an early settle at 12:00 CT.

**Adhoc / non-recurring full-day closures relevant to 1990–2022:**

| Date(s) | Event | Affected venues |
|---|---|---|
| **1994-04-27** | Richard Nixon funeral | NYSE closed; CME/NYMEX largely closed for floor |
| **2001-09-11 → 2001-09-14** | 9/11 attacks; NYSE closed Sept 11–14 (reopen Sept 17). NYMEX trading floor was four blocks from the WTC. Per EIA Natural Gas Weekly Update, week of Sept 17, 2001: *"Futures trading did not resume until Friday, and then only in an abbreviated session using the NYMEX ACCESS system. Regular, open-outcry trading resumed at around 11:30 AM today (Monday, September 17)."* | NYMEX (CL/HO/RB/NG) and COMEX (GC/SI/HG/PL): expect missing rows Sept 11–13; possibly an abbreviated/light row Sept 14. ES (and bonds): closed Sept 11–14. NQ (~not yet on E-mini Globex full-day): closed Sept 11–14. FESX: Eurex was open in Europe but liquidity collapsed. |
| **2004-06-11** | Reagan state funeral; declared National Day of Mourning. NYSE, Nasdaq, bond market, and CME group closed. | All US instruments missing 2004-06-11. FESX trades normally. |
| **2007-01-02** | Ford funeral. NYSE/Nasdaq closed entire day. **However**, NYMEX/COMEX electronic Globex trading was open. Per CNBC, "Most U.S. Markets Will Be Closed Tuesday for Ford's Funeral" (January 1, 2007): *"The NYMEX trading pits will be dark, but electronic trading of all NYMEX and COMEX division products through CME Globex will be available to energy and metals customers from 6:00 p.m. New York Time on Jan. 1 until 5:15 p.m. on Jan. 2."* | ES/NQ: missing. CL/GC/SI/HG/PL/HO/RB/NG: settle row **likely exists** (electronic). If your vendor uses NYSE/floor calendar, they may still drop the row. |

NYMEX floor closed; **NYMEX/COMEX electronic Globex stayed open** for energy and metals. Per Fox Business, "NYMEX Floor Trading Suspended due to Sandy, Electronic Trade Open" (Oct. 28, 2012), quoting CME spokesman Damon Leavall: *"Electronic trading at all CME markets will open at the regularly scheduled time at Globex and ClearPort, CME's online electronic platforms."* | ES/NQ: missing Oct 29 and Oct 30. CL/HO/RB/NG/GC/SI/HG/PL: rows may exist (electronic), volume low. FESX: trades normally. |
| **2018-12-05** | George H.W. Bush funeral. NYSE/Nasdaq/bond market closed. CME shuttered U.S.-based equity and interest-rate products. Behavior for NYMEX energy/COMEX metals is ambiguous in public sources. | ES/NQ: missing. CL/GC etc: likely missing but verify. |

### 3. Eurex / FESX holiday schedule
Eurex (XEUR; operating MIC for Eurex Deutschland) holiday rules for index products like FESX:

- **Full closures:** January 1, **Good Friday**, **Easter Monday**, **May 1 (Labour Day)**, **Christmas Day (Dec 25)**, **Boxing Day (Dec 26)**. (STOXX dissemination calendar: *"STOXX Eurex Calendar: every weekday except January 1st, Good Friday, Easter Monday, May 1st, December 24th, December 25th, December 26th, and December 31st"*.)
- **Early closes (around 12:30 CET):** Dec 24 (Christmas Eve) and Dec 31 (New Year's Eve). For daily OHLCV the row should *exist* but volume will be low.
- **Whit Monday / Pentecost Monday:** observed by Eurex through approximately 2007; dropped after; **occasionally re-instated** (2015 confirmed by Eurex circular #185982 because the day coincided with US Memorial Day and UK Late May Bank Holiday). Treat year-by-year: if you see FESX missing on a date that is Pentecost Monday, investigate further.
- **German Unity Day (Oct 3):** **not** a full closure for FESX (it is DAX-family only). Expect FESX rows on Oct 3 in normal years.

**Critical cross-calendar implication:** FESX will be **missing** on Easter Monday, May 1, and Dec 26 even though the US futures all trade those days. Conversely, US instruments will be missing on Thanksgiving, Independence Day, Memorial Day, Labor Day, and MLK Day even though FESX trades. This is the single biggest source of "missing for one instrument, present for others" gaps in a multi-venue dataset.

### 4. Why a daily futures row can be missing — full taxonomy
Sorted by typical frequency, with a programmatic detection rule for each.

| # | Cause | Detection rule |
|---|---|---|
| (a) | **Full-market holiday on the instrument's venue** | The instrument's calendar (`CMES`, `XEUR`, `CME_Equity`) says it is *not* a session day → `is_session` returns False. This will explain ~90%+ of weekday gaps. |
| (b) | **Exchange-specific holiday differing between venues** | Date is a session for some calendars but not others. Diagnostic: in the cross-sectional presence matrix, the row has gaps for *only* US-venue instruments or *only* fesx1s. |
| (c) | **Instrument inception / not-yet-listed** | The date is earlier than the instrument's first observed date in the data. ES: 1997-09; FESX: 1998-06; NQ: 1999-06. Flag and exclude *before* further classification. |
| (d) | **Instrument delisting / discontinuation** | Last observed date is materially before dataset end. None in this dataset (all 11 instruments active through 2022-06-30). |
| (e) | **Contract roll gap in a continuous series** | Adjacent OHLCV jumps abnormally (sometimes a 1-day gap is inserted by the vendor to mark a roll). Detection: rolling z-score on `close.pct_change()` is extreme on the day before a missing day. Rare for `*1s` continuous front-month series, which are usually stitched without gaps. |
| (f) | **Zero-volume day with vendor row dropped** | The date is a valid session by the calendar but the vendor drops it because volume = 0. Almost never happens for liquid futures like CL/GC/ES; can happen for thinly traded contracts very early in their life (e.g., NQ in 1999) or PL on certain days. Detection: cross-reference adjacent days' volume; if they are low, suspect liquidity. |
| (g) | **Vendor artifact / backfill / interpolation** | A row is present but *should not be*. The Sunday 2005-05-08 rows for gc1s, hg1s, si1s are this exact case — Sunday is never a session for COMEX, and the values look interpolated with zero/near-zero volume. Detection: day-of-week is Sat/Sun AND volume ≈ 0, OR OHLC values lack normal tick precision (non-multiples of the tick size). |
| (h) | **Weekend incorrectly present** | Subset of (g). Cheap detection: `df['date'].dt.dayofweek >= 5`. |
| (i) | **Source data gap / vendor outage** | A handful of weekdays missing across *many* instruments on the same date, where none of (a)–(h) apply. Investigate the vendor or news for that date. |
| (j) | **Low-liquidity instrument simply didn't trade** | Possible for PL or NQ in very early life; same detection as (f). For 2000+ all 11 instruments are liquid enough that this should not matter. |

**The cross-sectional diagnostic algorithm** the user asked for, stated formally:

```
For each candidate missing date D:
  N_missing(D) = number of instruments whose active span includes D but have no row on D
  N_active(D)  = number of instruments whose active span includes D
  ratio        = N_missing(D) / N_active(D)
  
  if ratio == 1.0          -> market-wide holiday or market-wide outage
  if 0 < ratio < 1.0:
      if missing set ⊆ {fesx1s}                       -> Eurex-only holiday
      if missing set ⊆ {US instruments}               -> US-wide holiday but Eurex trades
      if missing set ⊆ {es1s, nq1s}                   -> CME equity-only (Sandy 2012, Bush 2018)
      if missing set ⊆ {one instrument}               -> inception, roll, liquidity, or vendor
```

```python
"""
diagnose_missing_dates.py
-------------------------
Diagnose and classify missing dates in a daily OHLCV futures dataset
covering NYMEX/COMEX/CME and Eurex from 1990-01-02 to 2022-06-30.

Author : diagnostic template
Usage  : python diagnose_missing_dates.py
Outputs: prints and saves CSV summaries to ./diagnostics/

Required packages (install with pip):

"""

import os

from typing import Dict, List, Optional, Set, Tuple

import numpy as np
import pandas as pd

try:

except ImportError:

# Each instrument maps to:
#   - a primary calendar name (passed to pandas_market_calendars / exchange_calendars)
#   - a "venue group" used for the cross-sectional diagnostic
#
# Notes on choices:
#   - CME_Equity is purpose-built in pandas_market_calendars for the ES/NQ
#     E-mini futures and correctly handles the equity-only holiday
#     differences (e.g., Good Friday treatment).
#   - CMES is the unified CME Group calendar in exchange_calendars; it is the
#     best available proxy for NYMEX energies and COMEX metals because
#     pandas_market_calendars does NOT ship a dedicated NYMEX/COMEX calendar.
#   - XEUR covers Eurex (Euro STOXX 50 futures, FESX).

INSTRUMENT_MAP: Dict[str, Dict[str, str]] = {
    # NYMEX energies (unified CME calendar)
    "cl1s":   {"calendar": "CMES",       "venue": "NYMEX"},  # WTI crude
    "ho1s":   {"calendar": "CMES",       "venue": "NYMEX"},  # heating oil
    "rb1s":   {"calendar": "CMES",       "venue": "NYMEX"},  # RBOB gasoline
    "ng1s":   {"calendar": "CMES",       "venue": "NYMEX"},  # Henry Hub natgas
    # COMEX metals (unified CME calendar)
    "gc1s":   {"calendar": "CMES",       "venue": "COMEX"},  # gold
    "si1s":   {"calendar": "CMES",       "venue": "COMEX"},  # silver
    "hg1s":   {"calendar": "CMES",       "venue": "COMEX"},  # copper
    "pl1s":   {"calendar": "CMES",       "venue": "COMEX"},  # platinum
    # CME equity index (use the equity-specific calendar)
    "es1s":   {"calendar": "CME_Equity", "venue": "CME_EQ"}, # E-mini S&P 500
    "nq1s":   {"calendar": "CME_Equity", "venue": "CME_EQ"}, # E-mini Nasdaq-100
    # Eurex
    "fesx1s": {"calendar": "XEUR",       "venue": "EUREX"},  # Euro STOXX 50
}

# The library calendars are only reliable from roughly 2000 onward; for
# 1990-1999 we use a hardcoded US-futures holiday list to avoid false
# positives (e.g., the libraries may incorrectly mark MLK Mondays in
# 1990-1997 as non-sessions when NYMEX energy floor actually traded them).
LIBRARY_RELIABLE_START = pd.Timestamp("2000-01-01")


# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #
# SECTION 2. Hardcoded fallback holiday tables                       #
# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #
# These cover the period the library calendars don't reliably reach
# (pre-2000) and also encode the adhoc closures (9/11, Reagan, Ford,
# Sandy, Bush) so we can verify the library output.

# US futures full-day closures, observed by CME/NYMEX/COMEX as a unified
# group (post-2008) or aligned with NYSE/floor (pre-2008).
# Format: (YYYY-MM-DD, label).
US_FUTURES_HOLIDAYS_HARDCODED: List[Tuple[str, str]] = [
    # ---- Adhoc / non-recurring full-day closures ----
    ("1994-04-27", "Nixon state funeral"),
    ("2001-09-11", "9/11 attacks - market closed"),
    ("2001-09-12", "9/11 aftermath - market closed"),
    ("2001-09-13", "9/11 aftermath - market closed"),
    ("2001-09-14", "9/11 aftermath - market closed (NYMEX abbrev. ACCESS session)"),
    ("2004-06-11", "Reagan state funeral"),
    ("2007-01-02", "Ford state funeral (NYSE/CME equity closed; NYMEX/COMEX electronic open)"),
    ("2012-10-29", "Hurricane Sandy (NYSE + CME equity closed; NYMEX electronic open)"),
    ("2012-10-30", "Hurricane Sandy (NYSE + CME equity closed; NYMEX electronic open)"),
    ("2018-12-05", "George H.W. Bush state funeral"),
]

# Recurring rule-based US holidays 1990-2022.
def _build_us_recurring_holidays(year_start: int, year_end: int) -> Dict[pd.Timestamp, str]:
    """Build the recurring federal-style holiday calendar that CME/NYMEX/COMEX
    follow (with the historical caveats: MLK from 1998, Juneteenth from 2022).
    """
    out: Dict[pd.Timestamp, str] = {}
    from dateutil.relativedelta import relativedelta, MO, TH
    from dateutil.easter import easter
    from datetime import timedelta

    for y in range(year_start, year_end + 1):
        # New Year's Day (observed)
        nyd = _observed(date(y, 1, 1))
        out[pd.Timestamp(nyd)] = "New Year's Day"
        # MLK Day -- third Monday of January, from 1998 onward for exchanges
        if y >= 1998:
            mlk = date(y, 1, 1) + relativedelta(weekday=MO(+3))
            out[pd.Timestamp(mlk)] = "Martin Luther King Jr. Day"
        # Presidents' Day -- third Monday of February (NYSE since 1971;
        # NYMEX energy floor did not always close pre-2008 but post-2008 unified)
        pd_ = date(y, 2, 1) + relativedelta(weekday=MO(+3))
        out[pd.Timestamp(pd_)] = "Presidents' Day (Washington's Birthday)"
        # Good Friday -- Easter minus 2 days
        gf = easter(y) - timedelta(days=2)
        out[pd.Timestamp(gf)] = "Good Friday"
        # Memorial Day -- last Monday of May
        md = date(y, 5, 31) + relativedelta(weekday=MO(-1))
        out[pd.Timestamp(md)] = "Memorial Day"
        # Juneteenth -- from 2022, observed
        if y >= 2022:
            jt = _observed(date(y, 6, 19))
            out[pd.Timestamp(jt)] = "Juneteenth"
        # Independence Day (observed)
        ind = _observed(date(y, 7, 4))
        out[pd.Timestamp(ind)] = "Independence Day"
        # Labor Day -- first Monday of September
        lb = date(y, 9, 1) + relativedelta(weekday=MO(+1))
        out[pd.Timestamp(lb)] = "Labor Day"
        # Thanksgiving -- fourth Thursday of November
        tg = date(y, 11, 1) + relativedelta(weekday=TH(+4))
        out[pd.Timestamp(tg)] = "Thanksgiving Day"
        # Christmas (observed)
        xm = _observed(date(y, 12, 25))
        out[pd.Timestamp(xm)] = "Christmas Day"
    return out

def _observed(d: date) -> date:
    """If the date falls on Saturday, observe the previous Friday;
    if on Sunday, observe the following Monday. Matches US federal rule."""
    from datetime import timedelta
    if d.weekday() == 5:   # Saturday
        return d - timedelta(days=1)
    if d.weekday() == 6:   # Sunday
        return d + timedelta(days=1)
    return d

# Eurex / FESX recurring closures.
def _build_eurex_holidays(year_start: int, year_end: int) -> Dict[pd.Timestamp, str]:
    out: Dict[pd.Timestamp, str] = {}
    from dateutil.easter import easter
    from datetime import timedelta
    for y in range(year_start, year_end + 1):
        out[pd.Timestamp(date(y, 1, 1))] = "New Year's Day (Eurex)"
        e = easter(y)
        out[pd.Timestamp(e - timedelta(days=2))] = "Good Friday (Eurex)"
        out[pd.Timestamp(e + timedelta(days=1))] = "Easter Monday (Eurex)"
        out[pd.Timestamp(date(y, 5, 1))]  = "Labour Day (Eurex)"
        out[pd.Timestamp(date(y, 12, 25))] = "Christmas Day (Eurex)"
        out[pd.Timestamp(date(y, 12, 26))] = "Boxing Day (Eurex)"
        # Dec 24 and Dec 31 are early closes, not full closures -> not in this dict.
        # Whit Monday: was a closure ~1998-2007, then dropped, occasionally re-added.
        # We include 1998-2007 here as a conservative default; refine per year as needed.
        if 1998 <= y <= 2007:
            out[pd.Timestamp(e + timedelta(days=50))] = "Whit Monday (Eurex)"
        # 2015 was a one-off re-instatement.
        if y == 2015:
            out[pd.Timestamp(e + timedelta(days=50))] = "Whit Monday (Eurex, 2015 one-off)"
    return out


# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #
# SECTION 3. Build holiday set per calendar code                     #
# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #

@dataclass
class CalendarInfo:
    """Per-calendar information needed to classify dates."""
    name: str
    holidays: Dict[pd.Timestamp, str]     # date -> holiday label
    sessions: pd.DatetimeIndex            # the set of expected session dates
    early_closes: pd.DatetimeIndex        # for reference; NOT treated as missing

def build_calendar_info(cal_name: str,
                         start: pd.Timestamp,
                         end: pd.Timestamp) -> CalendarInfo:
    """Build sessions + holidays for one calendar code.

    Strategy:
      1) If the year is >= LIBRARY_RELIABLE_START AND mcal is installed,
         use the library to get the session DatetimeIndex.
      2) For pre-LIBRARY_RELIABLE_START years, generate a custom session
         index = weekdays minus our hardcoded holiday set.
      3) Stitch the two halves together.
    """
    # Step A: build the holiday name table from the hardcoded list. The
    # library will give us *which* days are sessions, but it does not give
    # us friendly holiday *names*, so we always build the name table here.
    if cal_name in ("CMES", "CME_Equity"):
        rule_hols = _build_us_recurring_holidays(start.year, end.year)
        # adhoc closures
        for d_str, label in US_FUTURES_HOLIDAYS_HARDCODED:
            rule_hols[pd.Timestamp(d_str)] = label
    elif cal_name == "XEUR":
        rule_hols = _build_eurex_holidays(start.year, end.year)
    else:
        raise ValueError(f"Unknown calendar code {cal_name}")

    # Step B: ask the library for sessions in the reliable window.
    sessions_lib = pd.DatetimeIndex([])
    early_closes_lib = pd.DatetimeIndex([])
    if HAVE_MCAL and end >= LIBRARY_RELIABLE_START:
        lib_start = max(start, LIBRARY_RELIABLE_START)
        try:
            cal = mcal.get_calendar(cal_name)
            sched = cal.schedule(start_date=lib_start.strftime("%Y-%m-%d"),
                                 end_date=end.strftime("%Y-%m-%d"))
            sessions_lib = pd.DatetimeIndex(sched.index.normalize())
            # Early closes: pandas_market_calendars exposes them on the schedule
            try:
                ec = cal.early_closes(schedule=sched)
                early_closes_lib = pd.DatetimeIndex(ec.index.normalize())
            except Exception:
                pass
        except Exception as exc:
            warnings.warn(f"Library failed for {cal_name}: {exc}; using fallback rule only.")

    # Step C: for the pre-library window, build sessions from rule.
    sessions_pre = pd.DatetimeIndex([])
    if start < LIBRARY_RELIABLE_START:
        pre_end = min(end, LIBRARY_RELIABLE_START - pd.Timedelta(days=1))
        all_wd = pd.bdate_range(start, pre_end)
        holiday_set = set(rule_hols.keys())
        sessions_pre = all_wd[~all_wd.isin(holiday_set)]

    # Step D: union
    sessions = sessions_pre.union(sessions_lib).sort_values()

    return CalendarInfo(
        name=cal_name,
        holidays=rule_hols,
        sessions=sessions,
        early_closes=early_closes_lib,
    )


# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #
# SECTION 4. Load the data                                           #
# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #

DATA_PATH = "/mnt/user-data/uploads/ohlcv_data.csv"

def load_data(path: str = DATA_PATH) -> pd.DataFrame:
    """Load the OHLCV file. Parse dates. Force numeric. Sort."""
    df = pd.read_csv(path)
    df["date"] = pd.to_datetime(df["date"]).dt.normalize()
    num_cols = ["open", "high", "low", "close", "volume", "open_interest"]
    for c in num_cols:
        df[c] = pd.to_numeric(df[c], errors="coerce")
    df = df.sort_values(["instrument", "date"]).reset_index(drop=True)
    return df


# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #
# SECTION 5. Per-instrument missing-date diagnostic                  #
# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #

def diagnose_instrument(df_inst: pd.DataFrame,
                         cal_info: CalendarInfo) -> pd.DataFrame:
    """For one instrument, return a DataFrame of every missing date with
    its classification."""
    inception = df_inst["date"].min()
    last      = df_inst["date"].max()

    # Expected = sessions within the instrument's active span
    expected = cal_info.sessions[(cal_info.sessions >= inception) &
                                  (cal_info.sessions <= last)]
    present  = pd.DatetimeIndex(df_inst["date"].unique())
    missing  = expected.difference(present)

    rows = []
    for d in missing:
        # already guaranteed to be inside (inception, last) and a session,
        # so the only "missing" reason if the holiday set doesn't claim it is
        # an unexplained vendor gap.
        label = cal_info.holidays.get(d, None)
        if label is not None:
            classification = "holiday"
        else:
            classification = "unexplained"
        rows.append({"date": d, "classification": classification,
                     "holiday_name": label or ""})
    return pd.DataFrame(rows)


# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #
# SECTION 6. Anomaly detection: weekends, zero-vol, non-finite       #
# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #

def detect_anomalous_rows(df: pd.DataFrame) -> pd.DataFrame:
    """Rows that should not exist or look like vendor artifacts."""
    flags = []
    is_weekend = df["date"].dt.dayofweek >= 5
    is_zero_vol = (df["volume"].fillna(0) == 0)
    is_nan_ohlc = df[["open", "high", "low", "close"]].isna().any(axis=1)

    if is_weekend.any():
        flags.append(df[is_weekend].assign(flag="weekend_row"))
    if (is_zero_vol & ~is_weekend).any():
        flags.append(df[is_zero_vol & ~is_weekend].assign(flag="zero_volume_weekday"))
    if is_nan_ohlc.any():
        flags.append(df[is_nan_ohlc].assign(flag="non_finite_ohlc"))

    if not flags:
        return pd.DataFrame(columns=list(df.columns) + ["flag"])
    return pd.concat(flags, ignore_index=True).drop_duplicates(
        subset=["date", "instrument", "flag"]
    )


# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #
# SECTION 7. Cross-sectional presence matrix                         #
# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #

def build_presence_matrix(df: pd.DataFrame,
                           instruments: List[str],
                           date_start: pd.Timestamp,
                           date_end: pd.Timestamp) -> pd.DataFrame:
    """Pivot to date x instrument boolean matrix.
    True = present in the dataset that day."""
    all_weekdays = pd.bdate_range(date_start, date_end)
    pivot = (df.assign(present=True)
               .pivot_table(index="date", columns="instrument",
                            values="present", aggfunc="any",
                            fill_value=False))
    pivot = pivot.reindex(all_weekdays, fill_value=False)
    return pivot[instruments]  # consistent column order


def classify_market_wide(presence: pd.DataFrame,
                          inceptions: Dict[str, pd.Timestamp]) -> pd.DataFrame:
    """For each date, count how many ACTIVE instruments are missing.
    "Active" means inception <= date."""
    out_rows = []
    for d, row in presence.iterrows():
        active = [k for k, inc in inceptions.items() if inc <= d]
        if not active:
            continue
        active_row = row[active]
        n_missing = (~active_row).sum()
        n_active  = len(active)
        if n_missing == 0:
            continue
        missing_set = set(active_row.index[~active_row.values])
        if n_missing == n_active:
            kind = "market_wide"
        elif missing_set <= {"fesx1s"}:
            kind = "eurex_only"
        elif missing_set <= {"es1s", "nq1s"}:
            kind = "cme_equity_only"
        elif "fesx1s" not in missing_set:
            kind = "us_only"
        else:
            kind = "mixed"
        out_rows.append({
            "date": d, "n_missing": n_missing, "n_active": n_active,
            "kind": kind, "missing_set": sorted(missing_set),
        })
    return pd.DataFrame(out_rows).sort_values("date")


# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #
# SECTION 8. Driver                                                  #
# ------------------------------------------------------------------ #

def main(out_dir: str = "diagnostics"):
    os.makedirs(out_dir, exist_ok=True)
    df = load_data()
    print(f"Loaded {len(df):,} rows; instruments={sorted(df['instrument'].unique())}")

    date_start = df["date"].min()
    date_end   = df["date"].max()
    print(f"Date range: {date_start.date()} -> {date_end.date()}")

    # ---- Anomalies ----
    anomalies = detect_anomalous_rows(df)
    anomalies.to_csv(f"{out_dir}/anomalous_rows.csv", index=False)
    print(f"\n[1] Anomalous rows flagged: {len(anomalies)}")
    if not anomalies.empty:
        print(anomalies[["date", "instrument", "volume", "flag"]].head(20).to_string(index=False))

    # ---- Build calendars ----
    cal_codes = sorted({m["calendar"] for m in INSTRUMENT_MAP.values()})
    cals: Dict[str, CalendarInfo] = {}
    for code in cal_codes:
        cals[code] = build_calendar_info(code, date_start, date_end)
        print(f"  built calendar {code}: {len(cals[code].sessions):,} sessions, "
              f"{len(cals[code].holidays):,} labelled holidays")

    # ---- Per-instrument missing-date diagnostics ----
    inceptions: Dict[str, pd.Timestamp] = {}
    per_inst_results: Dict[str, pd.DataFrame] = {}
    summary_rows = []
    for inst, meta in INSTRUMENT_MAP.items():
        di = df[df["instrument"] == inst]
        if di.empty:
            warnings.warn(f"No rows for {inst}; skipping")
            continue
        inceptions[inst] = di["date"].min()
        cal_info = cals[meta["calendar"]]
        miss = diagnose_instrument(di, cal_info)
        miss["instrument"] = inst
        miss["venue"] = meta["venue"]
        per_inst_results[inst] = miss
        summary_rows.append({
            "instrument": inst,
            "venue": meta["venue"],
            "calendar": meta["calendar"],
            "inception": inceptions[inst].date(),
            "n_present": di["date"].nunique(),
            "n_missing_total": len(miss),
            "n_missing_holiday": int((miss["classification"] == "holiday").sum()),
            "n_missing_unexplained": int((miss["classification"] == "unexplained").sum()),
        })

    per_inst_concat = pd.concat(per_inst_results.values(), ignore_index=True)
    per_inst_concat.to_csv(f"{out_dir}/missing_dates_per_instrument.csv", index=False)

    summary = pd.DataFrame(summary_rows)
    summary.to_csv(f"{out_dir}/summary_per_instrument.csv", index=False)
    print("\n[2] Per-instrument summary:")
    print(summary.to_string(index=False))

    # ---- Cross-sectional matrix ----
    instruments = list(INSTRUMENT_MAP.keys())
    presence = build_presence_matrix(df, instruments, date_start, date_end)
    presence.to_csv(f"{out_dir}/presence_matrix.csv")
    market_wide = classify_market_wide(presence, inceptions)
    market_wide.to_csv(f"{out_dir}/market_wide_classification.csv", index=False)

    print("\n[3] Cross-sectional missing-date classification counts:")
    print(market_wide["kind"].value_counts().to_string())

    print("\n[4] Top 20 dates missing for many instruments at once "
          "(candidate market-wide events):")
    top = market_wide.sort_values(["n_missing", "date"], ascending=[False, True]).head(20)
    print(top.to_string(index=False))

    print("\n[5] Unexplained missing dates (NOT in holiday table, "
          "after inception, on a calendar session):")
    unexpl = per_inst_concat[per_inst_concat["classification"] == "unexplained"]
    print(f"   total = {len(unexpl)}; by instrument:")
    print(unexpl["instrument"].value_counts().to_string())
    unexpl.to_csv(f"{out_dir}/unexplained_missing.csv", index=False)

    print(f"\nAll outputs written to ./{out_dir}/")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
```

### What the code does, section by section

- **Section 1** declares the ticker → calendar map. ES/NQ go to `CME_Equity` (purpose-built, models the daily 4:15–4:35 ET break and equity-only holiday differences). Everything else NYMEX/COMEX goes to `CMES` (the unified CME calendar). FESX goes to `XEUR`.
- **Section 2** is the **hardcoded fallback**. Two functions generate the rule-based recurring closures (US and Eurex) and a list literal contains adhoc closures (9/11, Reagan, Ford, Sandy, Bush). These are used both (a) as the *labels* (the libraries return sessions but not the friendly holiday name) and (b) as the *substitute* for 1990–1999 where the libraries are not reliable.
- **Section 3** stitches the library output (≥2000) with the rule-based output (<2000) to produce, per calendar code, a single `CalendarInfo` containing the expected session DatetimeIndex and the date→label dictionary.
- **Section 5** computes per-instrument missing dates: take the calendar's sessions inside the instrument's active span, subtract dates present in the data, classify each as `holiday` (name found) or `unexplained` (no name).
- **Section 6** detects vendor artifacts: weekend rows (catches the 2005-05-08 Sunday rows the user flagged), zero-volume weekdays, and non-finite OHLC.
- **Section 7** is the cross-sectional diagnostic the user explicitly asked for. The pivot table is `date × instrument → bool present`. The classifier looks at which instruments are missing on each missing date and labels it `market_wide`, `eurex_only`, `cme_equity_only`, `us_only`, or `mixed`.
- **Section 8** glues everything together and writes five CSVs plus a console summary.

### Expected output shape with the user's file
- ~9.4 holidays/yr × 32.5 years ≈ **305 expected holiday missings per instrument**, matching the user's observed 307–309 (the small surplus = inception offset and the 2001/2004/2012/2018 adhoc closures).
- Three anomalous Sunday 2005-05-08 rows for gc/hg/si will be flagged by `detect_anomalous_rows`.
- The market-wide classifier should produce ~210–220 `market_wide` rows (recurring US holidays the libraries also include in `CMES`), a handful of `eurex_only` rows (Easter Monday, May 1, Dec 26 — once FESX has inception), several `us_only` rows for US holidays that Eurex trades through, two `cme_equity_only` rows for Hurricane Sandy 2012-10-29/30, and the Bush 2018-12-05 closure.

## Recommendations

1. **Run the script above as-is** and look first at the **`unexplained_missing.csv`** output. Anything in that file is, by construction, a date that (a) is inside the instrument's active span, (b) is a session day on the instrument's exchange calendar, and (c) is not in the hardcoded holiday list. Each such date needs a manual judgment call: contract roll, vendor outage, or liquidity. Expect this list to be very short (single digits per instrument).
2. **Drop the 3 anomalous Sunday 2005-05-08 rows** for gc/hg/si before any modeling. They are vendor artifacts with zero volume and interpolated-looking floats; including them will bias any statistic that depends on calendar-day frequency.
3. **For 2007-01-02 (Ford funeral) and 2012-10-29/30 (Hurricane Sandy)**, check explicitly whether your CL/HO/RB/NG/GC/SI/HG/PL rows are present. CME Globex electronic trading continued for energy and metals on those days, so the *expected* behavior is rows present (low volume) for energies/metals but missing for ES/NQ. If your vendor dropped those rows, that is a vendor calendar choice and the script's `cme_equity_only` classification will surface it as an oddity.
4. **For 2022-06-20 (first observed Juneteenth)**, expect ES/NQ to be missing (full CME equity closure) but CL/GC may have an early-close row.
5. **If the library version you have raises `ValueError: out of bounds` when calling `.schedule(start_date='2000-01-01', ...)`** for CMES or XEUR, raise `LIBRARY_RELIABLE_START` to `pd.Timestamp("2002-01-01")` (the safer common-denominator). Conversely if it accepts 1995-01-01 without error, lower the bound — but spot-check against the hardcoded set for false negatives.
6. **Promotion path**: once `unexplained_missing.csv` is empty (or the residue is documented), persist `cals` to disk via pickle so future runs are deterministic without depending on library version drift.

**Thresholds that change these recommendations:**
- If `unexplained_missing.csv` has more than ~10 rows for a single instrument concentrated in one year, that year had a vendor outage — escalate to the data provider rather than treating the rows as legitimately missing.
- If the cross-sectional `kind` counts show **`mixed`** rows you cannot account for, you have a deeper venue-specific issue (e.g., NYMEX-only vs COMEX-only treatment that `CMES` is collapsing). In that case, supplement `CMES` with separate NYMEX/COMEX hardcoded lists.

## Caveats

- The `CMES` calendar in `exchange_calendars` is a **single unified calendar** for the entire CME Group (CME, CBOT, NYMEX, COMEX) and does **not** distinguish per-venue holiday differences that existed pre-2008 — most notably, the NYMEX energy floor's pre-1998 behavior on MLK Day and its pre-2008 behavior on Presidents' Day. The hardcoded fallback used here for 1990–1999 *does* apply MLK Day starting 1998 only, which is the consensus exchange-equity rule; if your vendor backfilled NYMEX CL data with NYSE-style closures in the early 1990s, the script may show "false positive" missings on those Mondays. Spot-check by counting missing Mondays in January 1990–1997 across CL/HO/NG.
- The `exchange_calendars` library's `bound_min` for `CMES` and `XEUR` should be confirmed at runtime by calling `xcals.get_calendar('CMES').bound_min`; the value `2000-01-01` used here is the typical default but can vary by version.
- **Whit Monday (Pentecost Monday)** Eurex treatment is *inconsistent*: observed through ~2007, dropped, then reinstated as a one-off in 2015 per Eurex circular #185982. The code's hardcoded list reflects this conservatively (1998–2007 + 2015) but if your FESX data shows trades on, e.g., 2005-05-16 Whit Monday, the line in `_build_eurex_holidays` should be removed for that year.
- **Hurricane Sandy 2012-10-30** behavior: CME equity index futures closed; NYMEX/COMEX electronic Globex stayed open, per Fox Business, "NYMEX Floor Trading Suspended due to Sandy, Electronic Trade Open" (Oct. 28, 2012), quoting CME spokesman Damon Leavall: *"Electronic trading at all CME markets will open at the regularly scheduled time at Globex and ClearPort, CME's online electronic platforms."* The hardcoded table marks 2012-10-29 and 2012-10-30 as US-futures closures, which is correct for ES/NQ but may incorrectly mark CL/GC as expected-missing when they in fact have an electronic settle. If your data has CL/GC rows on those dates, override by removing those entries from the hardcoded list for the NYMEX/COMEX subset.
- **The `holidays` package** offers `holidays.NYSE()` (per readthedocs.io/holidays) for US equities and `holidays.ECB()` for euro markets; we did **not** use it as the primary source because (a) it has no NYMEX/COMEX/CME futures calendar and (b) it does not model early closes. We mention it as a labelling fallback if `pandas_market_calendars` is unavailable.
- **Library version drift**: `pandas_market_calendars` introduced its CME family of futures calendars in v3.5 (released June 25, 2022) with the changelog entry *"Added product specific contract calendars for CME futures exchange. First calendars are the CME Agricultural and CME Equity calendars."* In that release, `CMEEquityExchangeCalendar` was redefined to no longer mirror NYSE. If you are pinned to an older version, the `CME_Equity` calendar may still resolve to a NYSE-equivalent — confirm by running `mcal.get_calendar('CME_Equity').holidays().holidays[:5]` and checking that the values look futures-specific (e.g., includes Good Friday).
