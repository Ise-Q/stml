## `deflation.py` — the one real risk (design detail)

- **DSR** (Bailey–López de Prado 2014): `V[SR̂] = (1/(T−1))·(1 − γ₃·SR̂ + ((γ₄−1)/4)·SR̂²)`; benchmark `SR₀ = √V_trials·[(1−γ)·Φ⁻¹(1−1/N) + γ·Φ⁻¹(1−1/(N·e))]` (γ = Euler–Mascheroni ≈ 0.5772); `DSR = Φ[(SR̂−SR₀)/√V[SR̂]]`. **The Φ⁻¹(1−1/N) expected-max term and the skew/kurtosis variance adjustment are the subtly-wrong spots — verify against a synthetic track of known (SR, N, skew, kurtosis), not plausibility.**
- **MinBTL** (Bailey et al. 2014): `minBTL ≈ 2·ln(N) / E[max SR]²`. Test the monotonicity + a known N.
- **CSCV-PBO** (Bailey et al. 2017): matrix `M` of (time-blocks × trials) performance; all `C(S,S/2)` IS/OOS partitions; `λ = logit(OOS rank of IS-best)`; `PBO = P(λ<0)`. Reuse only combinatorial index-generation. Trials matrix from the horse-race candidates' OOS series (tractable); the per-candidate **strategy-return** matrix is the richer-but-optional form — sequence PBO last so a clean DSR/MinBTL isn't held hostage.
- **N_eff**: `compute_spearman_distance_matrix` on the trial-performance vectors → `OptimalClusterer` → cluster count.
- **The "doesn't clear" path is primary, not the exception.** With a ~6-month OOS window the gate may straddle or fail across the N-range — that is the *expected, correct* honest-negative output. Report it plainly; do **not** tune the trial universe to clear.
